Project 01 — Multi-System
Operations Rebuild

A multi-location vehicle rental operator was scaling its fleet, investor base, and partner network — all at once, with no single source of truth. Here's the system I architected and built.

01
The Problem

Growth had outrun the operation's memory.

Every part of the business lived in someone's head, a spreadsheet, or a group chat. No single source of truth — and no way for leadership to see the business without asking someone to go find out.

  • ✕ No real-time visibility into fleet profitability, utilization, or collections — leadership found out about problems after they'd already cost money.
  • ✕ Vehicle handoff relied entirely on a person remembering every step, with no record if something was missed.
  • ✕ Investor, partner, and renter relationships tangled in the same pipeline — no separation of data or process.
  • ✕ Nothing was logged. Payment failures, stalled pickups, missed follow-ups — no trace of why.
02
The Architecture

Six systems, one source of truth.

A from-scratch architecture — one system of record per function, data flowing one direction into an executive layer, zero manual re-entry anywhere in the chain.

What I Did

My specific contribution
to the build.

On a project with multiple workstreams and a backend dev team running in parallel, my scope centred on the executive layer, the pickup system, and keeping the whole build honest against what was actually signed off.

Dashboard Architecture

Designed and built the ten-KPI executive dashboard from scratch — status logic, automation rules, and the CEO-facing view — so leadership could assess the entire operation in under two minutes.

Workflow Engineering

Engineered the automated pickup workflows in GHL — vehicle auto-fill logic, gated entry conditions, and full event logging — removing manual data entry from the handoff process entirely.

Forms & Funnel Builds

Designed and built the intake forms and multi-step funnels behind the lead-gen system — from field-level validation to the automated tagging and routing that fires on submission.

Risk Catch

Ran a deep architectural review that surfaced a missing link between renter and vehicle records — a gap that would have caused silent reporting errors downstream. Flagged and scoped the fix before it shipped.

Scope Discipline

Managed delivery against a signed technical scope through a fast-moving client relationship — triaging new requests into future phases instead of letting scope creep erode the build.
